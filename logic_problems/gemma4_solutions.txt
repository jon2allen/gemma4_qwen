### **Bonus Problem: Fallacy Identification (Logical Fallacies)**

**Solution:**
Undistributed Middle

**Reasoning:**
The argument structure is:
1. All Cats (C) are Mammals (M).
2. Some Mammals (M) are Dogs (D).
3. Therefore, Some Cats (C) are Dogs (D).

In a valid syllogism, the middle term (Mammals, M) must be "distributed" at least once—meaning the premise must refer to *all* members of that category.

In Premise 2 ("Some Mammals are Dogs"), we only know about a *subset* of mammals; we do not know anything about all mammals. The overlap between Mammals and Dogs could occur entirely outside the group of Cats. Since the middle term (Mammals) is not distributed, the argument is logically unsound.

**Confidence Level:** High
